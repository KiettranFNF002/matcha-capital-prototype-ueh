## FINANCIAL DIAGNOSIS

<!-- image -->

2025 shows a large gap between accounting profit and cash generation, analysis indicates a quality-of-earnings warning for DRG:

- 1.Gross margin fell from 25% (2024) to 16% (2025) (COGS/Net Revenue up to 84%), meaning less buffer to absorb operating-cycle friction.
- 2.Profit vs cash decouples (earnings quality down): NPAT rises to VND 197bn (from VND 122bn), yet Net Operating Cash Flow flips to -VND 77bn (from +VND 168bn). Cash conversion deteriorates from 1.38x to -0.39x. This suggests 2025 profit is not cash-backed.
- 3.The cash conversion cycle lengthened from 73.81 to 85.73 days (DSO + DIO DPO), signaling more cash tied up to run the business.
- 4.In the cash flow bridge, underlying operating cash capacity was positive, but the swing is dominated by 'Other operating payments' of -VND 258bn, which is the single largest driver pushing Operating Cash Flow to -VND 77bn.

This is a year where (1) margins compressed, (2) the cash cycle lengthened, and (3) a large 'other operating payments' cash outflow hit, resulting in negative operating cash despite higher profit. Underwriting should treat and discount 2025 NPAT as lower-quality.

* The 2025 financial statement is unaudited.

## PROBLEM STATEMENT AND NEXT STEP

## 1. PROBLEM STATEMENT

DRG faces a structural financing mismatch:

Long-term plantation assets are funded by unstable cash flows and short-term capital sources.

Revenue and operating cash flow fluctuate with rubber price cycles, weakening cash flow predictability and reducing lenders' confidence in providing long-term financing.

## 2. WHY THIS PROBLEM IS STRATEGICALLY IMPORTANT

- Refinancing risk persists as long-term assets rely on rolling short-term liabilities.
- Operational contraction risk emerges when funding tightens, limiting plantation renewal and expansion.
- Credit perception deteriorates, reinforcing conservative lending behaviour from banks.
- Without structural adjustment, growth becomes cyclical rather than sustainable.
- The core issue lies in structural cash flow and financing mismatch rather than operational decline.
