Jobs to Be Done:
 Là một lăng kính (góc nhìn) để hiểu các động lực cơ bản thúc đẩy quyết định của khách hàng.
Lý thuyết Jobs to Be Done (JTBD)
JTBD là một khung phân tích để hiểu hành vi khách hàng.


Khách hàng không mua sản phẩm dựa trên nhân khẩu học hoặc đặc điểm của họ.


Thay vào đó, họ “thuê” (hire) một sản phẩm hoặc dịch vụ để thực hiện một “công việc” (job) cụ thể —
 tức là một nhiệm vụ hoặc sự tiến bộ mà họ muốn đạt được để tiến tới một mục tiêu hoặc khát vọng trong những hoàn cảnh cụ thể.

Xây dựng JTBD cần phải chú ý 3 yếu tố
Functional
 The financial or operational objective to accomplish
Example:
 “Ensure suppliers are paid on time without increasing short-term borrowing”
Emotional
 How the decision-maker (e.g., CFO, Finance Director, CEO…) feels when facing business friction
Example:
 “I want to feel confident that liquidity risks are under control.”
Social
 How the firm or manager wants to be perceived
Example:
 “I want to maintain credibility with owners, lenders, and auditors.”


Practical formula
Instead of using the traditional User Story (“As a [user], I want to…”), JTBD uses the Job Story to capture the context of the struggle:
When I [Situation], I want to [Action/Solution], so that I can [Expected Outcome].
Example:
 “When I am commuting on a long, boring drive, I want something to snack on for a long time, so that I can stay awake and the journey feels less tedious.”


Các bước để làm JTBD
Step 1: Define the Core Job
 What progress is the customer trying to make?
 Note: Avoid mentioning your specific product or solution here.
Step 2: Context & Circumstances
 When does this job occur? Where? With whom?
 What triggers this need in their daily life?
Step 3: Barriers & Pains
 What is preventing them from completing the job currently?
 What are the risks, frustrations, or trade-offs they are forced to make?
Step 4: Current Solutions & Trade-offs
 How are they solving the problem right now (or which competitor's product are they “hiring”)?
 What are the shortcomings of that current solution?
Step 5: Desired Outcomes (Success Metrics)
 What criteria do they use to measure success?
 (Is it faster? Cheaper? More peace of mind?)
Step 6: The Four Forces of Progress
 This is a unique element of JTBD that analyzes the “tug-of-war” in the customer's mind:
Push: Frustration with the current solution.


Pull: The attractiveness of the new solution (your product).


Anxiety: The fear or uncertainty of switching to something new.


Habit: The comfort and inertia of the old way of doing things.

Example 2
“Pains”: Operational Struggles and Financial Risks
1. Manual Drudgery & Errors
 Manual entry from paper/PDF invoices causes errors and duplicate payments. Manual approval processes get lost, leading to late payments.
2. Lack of Visibility
 Not knowing exact cash in different accounts (data fragmentation). Not knowing when customers will pay, leading to high DSO and working capital pressure.
3. Cost and Risk
 High transaction fees, especially international. Risk of Invoice Fraud or Business Email Compromise (BEC).
4. Siloed Systems
 Accounting software doesn’t talk to the bank. Staff must “copy-paste” data, wasting time.

“Gains”: Redefining Success in the Digital Era
80% – Efficiency Gain
 Reduce data entry time through automation.
70% – Faster Close
 Reduce month-end close from 10 days to 3 days.
85% – Forecast Accuracy
 Cash flow forecasting to support decision-making.
Additional Gains: Capture Early Payment Discounts, optimize working capital, reduce borrowing costs, earn cashback from corporate card usage, and gain real-time spend reporting by department/project.

Example 2
After understanding the customer, students design the solution (Left side of VPC). This is where the “Idea for a Suitable Solution” is formed.
Products and Services: Modern Fintech Solution Structure
SaaS Platform
 Web/mobile interface for users to enter data, view reports, and approve transactions.
Core Tech
 AI/ML for fraud detection, OCR for reading invoices.
Payment Rails
 Payment gateway integration, bank linking (API Banking), Virtual Card issuance.
Integration Ecosystem
 Ready-made connectors with popular ERPs (QuickBooks, SAP, Oracle, Xero).

Pain Relievers: Automation and Compliance
Solving Manual Drudgery
 Use OCR and Machine Learning to automatically extract invoice data and match against Purchase Orders (PO) and Goods Received Notes (GRN) — automated “3-way matching”.
Solving Siloed Systems
 Use two-way sync APIs so payments made on the app automatically book into accounting software.
Solving Fraud Risk
 Multi-level Approval Workflows on mobile and Single-use Virtual Cards help strictly control every dollar spent.

Gain Creators: Working Capital Optimization
Financial Benefits
 Dynamic Discounting or Supply Chain Finance allows buyers to pay suppliers early to receive a discount (e.g., 2% off for immediate payment), turning Accounts Payable from a cost center into a profit center.
Strategic Insight
 Real-time Spend Analytics Dashboards help CFOs see abnormal spending trends or remaining budget for projects without waiting for month-end reports.
